Mars and Venus are geologically active

Mar's Results for NASA's InSight Lander

On February 24, 2020, the researchers released the intital results for NASA's InSight lander on Mars. Covering the first 10 months of the mission, the findings have stated with the conclusion that Mars is seismically active. There were 174 marsquakes recorded from Febuary- September 2019 and the scientists were able to trace two of the strongest to Cerberus Fossae. Suzanne Smrekar, who is the deputy principal investigator for Insight has stated that "this young region has undergone volcanism and other geologic changes in the last 20 million to 25 million years." She also stated that Mars' surface is on average several billion years old.

Venus: The Second Active Planet

On July 20, 2021, a paper in Nature Geoscience added evidence that there was recent volcanic activity on Venus. The researchers at ETH Zurich, led by Anna Gulcher, looked at the circular features called coronae, which scientists think form when the faults develop around areas where rising magma lifts up the surface. The team has concluded that at least 37 of Venus' large coronae are still evolving, which indiciates that it is geologically active.
